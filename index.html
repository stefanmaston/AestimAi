Du ut des

I give, so you can give — the foundation of all human economy

Since the beginning, people have traded goods and services with one another. Barter — du ut des in Latin — is the original language of the economy. Yet we lack something fundamental: a universal measure of value. We have the metre for length, the kilogram for mass, the second for time. But for value? Nothing.

AestimAi is built to fill that gap.

Value has no "metre"

📏

Physical quantities have universal measures

A metre is a metre in Tokyo, Lagos and Stockholm. A kilogram weighs the same in every market. Scientific and technical systems work globally because of these universal reference points.

❓

Value has no such measure

An hour of work may be worth $15 in one country and $150 in another. A kilo of bread costs three times more depending on where you are. Currencies fluctuate, inflation erodes, and there is no neutral reference value to compare against.

⊙

UCI — Universal Coin Index — is the missing measure. A neutral reference point for exchange, independent of national currency, political decisions or inflation.

What is UCI?

UCI is not a currency. It is an index — a reference unit that measures the real exchange value of goods, services, energy, time and resources. Just as a thermometer does not create heat but measures it, UCI does not create value — it makes value visible and measurable.

⚖️

Neutral

Not tied to any single currency, nation or institution. UCI is a public good.

🌍

Universal

The same reference value works for a trade in Malmö and one in Nairobi. No exchange rates, no intermediaries.

📊

Index-based

Value builds organically from real exchanges, valuations and transactions — like a thermometer calibrated by the market itself.

🔍

Transparent

All valuations, transactions and index calculations are open and verifiable. No hidden algorithms.

🤝

Cooperative

The system is owned by its users. Everyone who values and trades helps make the index more precise.

♻️

Circular

Designed for an economy where resources circulate rather than being discarded. Barter as a system choice, not a fallback.

Why does this matter?

01

Fairer trades

When both parties in a trade use the same reference index, the power asymmetry disappears where one party holds "hard currency" and the other does not. UCI creates common ground.

02

Independent of currency fluctuations

Prices in UCI reflect real exchange value — not central bank rate decisions or speculators' positions. A service worth 10 UCI today is worth roughly 10 UCI tomorrow.

03

Makes the invisible visible

Housework, care, local knowledge, ecosystem services — everything market price misses. UCI can value what traditional economics chooses not to see.

04

Enables large-scale barter

Classic barter only works one-to-one and requires both parties to happen to have exactly what the other needs. UCI solves this with a shared index value — you trade with the whole network, not just one person.

05

ESG and sustainability data

CO₂ credits, biodiversity units, water rights — sustainability-related values are notoriously hard to compare. UCI offers a common reference frame for valuing environmental impact alongside economic outcomes.

06

Cooperative identity and trust

Through the AE ID system, each participant can build a verified reputation that travels with them — independent of platform, country or currency. Trust as infrastructure.
